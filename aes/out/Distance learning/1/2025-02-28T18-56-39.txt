**Distance Learning: An Overview**

Distance learning is a method where students learn without going to school every day. Instead, they attend classes remotely, often from home or another location via the internet.

One of the main advantages is flexibility. Students can study at any time that suits them, allowing for better time management and personal responsibilities. This remote access also makes education more accessible to people with busy schedules or those in remote areas.

However, there are challenges. Some students might find it harder to stay focused without a physical classroom setting. Additionally, group activities and social interactions can be missed out on, which might affect learning outcomes.

Despite these issues, distance learning has become feasible today with the help of technology like internet connectivity and online resources. While not everyone may have equal access or face similar challenges, many students find it beneficial for balancing work and education.
