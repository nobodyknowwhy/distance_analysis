Distance learning refers to the process of acquiring educational knowledge through online platforms or digital devices without being physically present in a traditional classroom. The use of technology has made education more accessible than ever before, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

One significant advantage is the flexibility it offers. Students can learn at their own pace and attend classes when convenient, which helps them balance school with work or family responsibilities. Additionally, distance learning allows students to access a wider range of courses from various universities worldwide, enabling them to specialize in areas they find fascinating. This accessibility often makes education more affordable than traditional on-campus programs.

However, there are challenges as well. Communication becomes harder without face-to-face interactions; students and teachers rely on emails or video calls instead. The pandemic has accelerated the shift towards digital education, raising questions about its long-term role in traditional education systems.
