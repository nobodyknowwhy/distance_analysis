Distance learning is a method of studying that occurs online without face-to-face meetings between students and teachers. It allows learners to access educational content from home or any location with internet connectivity.

In this approach, courses are often delivered through platforms like Moodle or Zoom (though some may be more accurately named), which provide video lectures, reading materials, and quizzes to assess understanding. Communication with instructors typically occurs via emails or video calls, enhancing accessibility for students who might otherwise lack such opportunities.

One challenge is the potential isolation that comes with learning remotely, as students miss out on visual cues during lessons and may find it harder to engage actively in discussions without peers nearby. Additionally, motivation can sometimes be lower due to the absence of immediate feedback from classmates.

Despite these challenges, distance learning offers flexibility, allowing students to review materials at their convenience and access resources that might not otherwise be available, such as specialized courses or expert teachings from other countries.

Overall, while it presents some difficulties, distance learning provides a valuable alternative for those unable to attend traditional classes, offering both convenience and diverse educational opportunities.
