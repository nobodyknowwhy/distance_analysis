**Distance Learning**

Distance learning refers to the practice of studying at an educational institution while physically separated from the school, typically through internet-based platforms. In recent years, this method has become increasingly popular due to its flexibility and accessibility.

One of the primary aspects of distance learning is that it allows students to attend classes without needing to be physically present at a traditional classroom or school. This can be beneficial for students who are unable to attend school regularly, such as those with physical disabilities or other commitments. Additionally, distance learning often provides more flexibility in terms of scheduling and pacing.

However, distance learning also has its challenges. Another challenge may be technical difficulties that arise when accessing online platforms, such as connectivity issues or login problems. Furthermore, students may find it harder to stay motivated without the structure and immediate feedback provided by traditional classroom settings.

In conclusion, distance learning has both benefits and challenges. While it offers flexibility and convenience for many students, it also presents unique obstacles that require careful navigation.
