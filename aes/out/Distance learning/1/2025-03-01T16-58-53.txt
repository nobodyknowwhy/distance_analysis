In recent years, distance learning has emerged as a revolutionary approach to education, transforming how people gain knowledge from the comfort of their homes or other locations. Distance learning operates primarily through online platforms like Moodle and Zoom, where students engage with course materials at their own pace, often completing assignments and communicating via emails and chat functions. This method allows for a high degree of autonomy, catering to various schedules and personal commitments.

One of the most significant advantages is flexibility, enabling students to study from anywhere without commuting. This accessibility reduces financial burdens as schools typically don't require physical locations or transportation, with many courses being free to audit. However, distance learning also has its drawbacks. Without face-to-face interaction, some students may find engagement lower compared to traditional settings.
