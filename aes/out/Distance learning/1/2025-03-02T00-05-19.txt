Distance learning is a method of education where students can study at home or in another place without meeting other students and teachers face-to-face.

It has been growing in popularity over the past few years because of technology advancements and the need for more flexible education. Originally, distance learning was used for special needs students who couldn’t attend school due to physical disabilities or health issues. Now it’s also becoming a common way for people to learn new skills or go back to school after taking a break.

One of the main challenges with distance learning is that students have to be self-disciplined and motivated because they don’t interact with their teachers or classmates as much. Also, not everyone has access to good internet or technology at home, which can make it harder for some people to keep up.

Despite these challenges, distance learning has its benefits. It allows people to learn from the comfort of their own homes, which can be helpful if they have other commitments like work or family responsibilities. Plus, students can watch lessons again if they miss something or ask questions at any time.
