**Distance Learning: An Introduction**

Distance learning refers to a method of education where students do not attend classes physically at a school or university. Instead, they access educational content online through various platforms such as websites or mobile apps. These platforms may include video lectures, interactive lessons, and virtual classrooms.

Students enrolled in distance learning courses can attend class sessions from home or any location with internet access. They can also interact with their teachers and classmates remotely. This method of education has become increasingly popular due to its flexibility and convenience for students who cannot attend classes in person.

One advantage of distance learning is the ability to study at one's own pace, allowing students to complete assignments and exams when they are ready. Additionally, it provides access to a wide range of courses from universities around the world, which may not be available locally.

However, distance learning can also present challenges such as maintaining focus without face-to-face interaction with instructors or peers. It requires self-discipline and time management skills to keep up with coursework effectively.

In conclusion, distance learning has revolutionized education by offering flexibility and accessibility to a broader range of students. While it may require some adjustments in study habits, it is an effective way to continue learning even when traditional classroom settings are not possible.
