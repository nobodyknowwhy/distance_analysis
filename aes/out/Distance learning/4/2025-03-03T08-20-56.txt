Distance learning has emerged as an innovative approach to education, revolutionizing the way students acquire knowledge in today's interconnected world. Originating from the need for accessible education during historical events like World War II, it has since evolved with technological advancements.

One of the primary advantages is convenience. Accessibility remains a significant factor in distance learning. It opens doors for individuals living in remote areas who may not have access to traditional schools. Additionally, it benefits those with disabilities by enabling them to attend school without moving physically, such as students with mobility issues.
