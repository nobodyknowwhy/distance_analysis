**Distance Learning: A New Approach to Education**

Distance learning refers to an educational method where students study at home or remotely through structured programs. It has gained popularity in recent years due to its flexibility and convenience. The COVID-19 pandemic further boosted its adoption, allowing many schools and colleges to switch gears quickly.

One significant advantage of distance learning is its cost-effectiveness for families. Students can attend institutions that are otherwise inaccessible by bus or car. Additionally, it offers flexibility, enabling students to study at their own pace and on their own schedule, which suits those with multiple commitments.

However, distance learning also has challenges. Students miss out on face-to-face interactions with teachers and peers, which can hinder collaborative learning and immediate feedback. Without a physical classroom, maintaining engagement or social interaction might be challenging for some learners.
