Distance learning is a modern educational approach where students attend classes remotely, typically through the internet. It has gained popularity due to technological advancements and events like the COVID-19 pandemic, which accelerated its adoption.

This method allows students to attend lessons from home or other locations, offering flexibility for those with demanding schedules. It also provides convenience, as students can access course materials anytime and anywhere via platforms like Moodle or Zoom.

However, distance learning presents challenges such as technical issues, which might prevent reliable internet access at home or device malfunctions. Additionally, without face-to-face interaction, students may struggle with time management, isolation, and feeling disconnected from peers during group activities.

Despite these hurdles, distance learning is here to stay due to its flexibility, but it requires students to adapt by managing their time effectively, seeking technical support when needed, and staying engaged in their studies. This approach not only addresses the limitations of traditional classrooms but also continues to evolve with technological progress.
