**Distance Learning: A Brief Overview**

Distance learning refers to the educational method where students attend classes remotely via technology. It has been around since the early 20th century but gained significant popularity with the advent of the internet in the late 1990s and early 2000s.

In traditional classrooms, students meet regularly under the supervision of an instructor. However, distance learning allows students to learn from home or other locations through online platforms. These platforms facilitate communication via emails and forums, enabling interaction with instructors and peers outside the classroom.

One advantage of distance learning is its flexibility, allowing students to attend classes at their own pace. Additionally, it provides access to a wide range of courses offered globally by various institutions. However, challenges such as isolation and technical issues can arise, which might affect student engagement.

Despite these challenges, distance learning has proven beneficial for students seeking remote education or those with personal commitments that make traditional classroom settings difficult to attend. Its global accessibility makes it an appealing option for diverse learners worldwide.
