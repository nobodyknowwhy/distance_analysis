**Distance Learning**

Distance learning refers to a method of education where students do not meet in a traditional classroom setting. Instead, they access learning materials online or through other digital platforms. This type of learning has become increasingly popular in recent years, especially with the rise of technology and the demands of modern life.

One of the main advantages of distance learning is its flexibility. Students can attend classes from home, work, or even travel around the world as long as they have access to internet connectivity. This makes it an ideal option for people who are juggling multiple responsibilities, such as working full-time while studying. However, distance learning also has its challenges. Without face-to-face interaction with instructors and peers, students may struggle to stay motivated and engaged. Another benefit is that distance learning allows students to access courses taught by experts from around the globe. This can be especially valuable for students interested in specialized fields such as international relations or advanced technology, where opportunities for study may be limited in their local area. Despite these advantages, some argue that distance learning lacks the personal interaction and immediate feedback that on-campus classes provide.
