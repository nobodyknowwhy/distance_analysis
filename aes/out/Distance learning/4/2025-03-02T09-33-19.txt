Distance learning is an educational approach where students engage with their courses remotely, often through technology like online platforms. It offers flexibility and convenience, allowing students to learn at their own pace without traditional classroom constraints.

This method has expanded access to education worldwide, particularly during the pandemic, which accelerated its adoption. Many countries now utilize it for both undergraduate and postgraduate studies, addressing diverse learning needs.

However, challenges such as isolation can arise due to the removal of face-to-face interaction between students and teachers. Additionally, distractions from technology use or lack of self-discipline may hinder academic performance.

Despite these issues, distance learning remains a valuable tool for enhancing educational accessibility. It bridges geographical gaps and provides tailored support to individual learning styles and paces, marking an essential adaptation in modern education systems.
