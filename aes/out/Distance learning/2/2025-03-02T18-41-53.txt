**Distance Learning: An Overview**

Distance learning refers to studying online from home instead of going to a traditional classroom. It's become more popular due to technological advancements, making remote education accessible to many people. During the pandemic, many schools and universities switched to online classes, increasing its popularity.

Those choosing distance learning often appreciate its convenience and flexibility. They can study at home without needing transportation or time, allowing them to manage their schedules effectively.

Online courses typically use platforms like Moodle or Zoom. These platforms facilitate structured learning with elements such as lectures, assignments, and discussions, enabling students to engage with the material at their own pace.

While distance learning offers independence for self-paced learning, it also presents challenges. Without face-to-face interaction with instructors or peers, maintaining motivation can be difficult.

In conclusion, distance learning broadens accessibility by eliminating the need for physical classrooms but may have its drawbacks, such as reduced interaction and potential isolation.
