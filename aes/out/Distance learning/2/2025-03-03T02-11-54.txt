Distance learning refers to a method of education where students engage with their courses and interact with teachers and peers through electronic platforms or the internet. It allows learning from home, often at one's own pace, which is advantageous for individuals with work or family commitments.

One significant benefit of distance learning is its flexibility. Students can access course materials anytime and anywhere, fitting study into their schedules effectively. This adaptability makes it a preferred choice for many.

However, distance learning also presents challenges. Technical issues, such as unstable internet connections, can disrupt learning. Additionally, without face-to-face interactions, students might feel isolated or miss out on beneficial peer discussions.

Another challenge is the need for self-discipline. Without direct supervision from teachers and classmates, maintaining focus and completing assignments becomes more difficult. Despite these hurdles, distance learning broadens access to education and promotes self-discipline among learners.
