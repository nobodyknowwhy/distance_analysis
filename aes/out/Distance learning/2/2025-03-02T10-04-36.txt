Distance learning is an educational method where students study without being physically present in a traditional classroom. It typically involves studying online through the internet or other digital platforms. This approach has become more common during recent events due to the pandemic, allowing people worldwide to access education from home.

One of the main advantages of distance learning is its flexibility. Students can attend classes at their own pace and schedule, which is especially helpful for those with busy lives or health issues that prevent them from attending school in person. It also offers accessibility to educational resources for students who live far from schools or have physical disabilities.

However, there are challenges too. Without direct interaction with teachers and classmates, it can be harder to stay motivated and engaged. Communication might rely on forums or emails, which could be less effective than face-to-face conversations. Additionally, managing study materials and staying disciplined without supervision can be difficult.

Despite these challenges, distance learning has its benefits, such as providing a more flexible schedule and access to additional resources that may not be available locally. Overall, while it requires careful planning, distance learning is a valuable method for many students who need alternative ways to continue their education.
