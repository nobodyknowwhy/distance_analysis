Distance learning is when you study without attending a school, often from home or another location online. It became more popular during the pandemic due to health concerns and the inability to attend classes in person. Using computers and the internet, students can access lessons through videos, quizzes, and assignments.

One benefit is flexibility—students can learn at their own pace and choose when to study. Online resources are abundant, offering a variety of materials. However, challenges include difficulty focusing without supervision and potential issues with internet connectivity. Communication may also be challenging if friends aren't nearby.

While distance learning offers convenience for some, it's not suitable for everyone. Motivation and self-discipline are crucial. Overall, while useful for certain individuals, it comes with its own set of challenges that traditional schooling doesn’t have.
