Distance learning is an outcome of technological advancement, allowing education to reach students from various locations. It involves learning online, often through computers or devices, enabling flexibility with time and access to materials anytime.

In this system, students attend classes remotely, either at home or other places via internet access. This method offers convenience for those balancing work or family commitments, providing access to courses from distant universities.

One advantage is the flexibility it provides. Students can manage their schedules around work or family duties, offering a comfortable learning environment outside traditional classroom settings. Additionally, online resources allow access to materials anytime, enhancing self-paced study.

However, challenges exist. Staying focused without face-to-face interaction and managing time effectively are crucial. Technical issues, such as poor internet connectivity, can hinder learning progress. Moreover, isolation from peers may affect social engagement and motivation.

Despite these hurdles, distance learning has significantly impacted modern education, offering a valuable alternative to conventional methods. Its adaptability and accessibility make it an essential part of contemporary educational landscape.
