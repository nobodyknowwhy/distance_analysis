Here’s an example of a distance learning composition written at an English level 2 standard:

---

Distance learning is a way to study without going to school or university in person. Instead, you can learn online from home or anywhere else with internet connection.

One big advantage of distance learning is that it gives people the chance to study even if they live far away from universities. This is especially good for students who have jobs or family responsibilities and cannot go to school regularly.

Distance learning also allows people in different countries to study together. For example, students from China and the United States can take the same class online and learn from each other’s experiences.

However, distance learning can be hard because you need to stay focused without a teacher around. Sometimes it is easy to forget what you are supposed to do or not remember what you learned last week. You also have to work harder on your own if someone is not available to help.

Another problem with online learning is that sometimes the technology, like computers and internet, doesn’t work well. If you can’t connect to the class website or your computer breaks down, it can be very frustrating.

Distance learning can take time because you have to watch videos, read books, and write essays on your own. It also takes longer than regular school because you cannot ask a teacher as often.

In the end, distance learning is useful for many people but needs good technology and self-discipline. With these things, it can work just like regular school.
