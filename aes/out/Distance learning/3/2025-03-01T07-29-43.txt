**Understanding Distance Learning**

Distance learning is a way people learn through computers or websites without meeting at a traditional school. It’s useful for students who can’t go to school in person.

It uses various tools like websites and software to teach. Some methods are live interactions, while others allow independent study with online materials.

One benefit is flexibility; students can choose when to study. They save time on traveling since they can learn at home. Plus, revisiting lessons as needed helps understand better.

However, challenges exist too. Not everyone has good internet or devices, making it hard for some to participate. Communication might be difficult without face-to-face meetings, which can reduce engagement compared to traditional classes.

Overall, distance learning is growing in popularity because more people benefit from it. It offers flexibility and convenience but faces issues with access and interaction.
