Distance learning refers to the educational method where students engage in studying through non-conventional means such as electronic resources and communication tools, rather than traditional face-to-face classroom instruction. It allows students to access courses from home or other locations, making it a popular alternative for those unable to attend regular classes due to health issues, work commitments, or geographical distances.

One of the key advantages is its flexibility; students can learn at their own pace and revisit complex topics as needed. Additionally, distance learning broadens access to education by enabling individuals from diverse locations and backgrounds to participate in academic programs. However, challenges such as maintaining focus without direct supervision, limited face-to-face interaction with peers and instructors, and technical issues like internet connectivity can affect the learning experience.

In summary, while distance learning offers flexibility and global educational opportunities, it also presents its own set of challenges that students and educators need to address to ensure effective learning outcomes.
