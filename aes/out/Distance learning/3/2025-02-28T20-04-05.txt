Distance learning has become a prevalent method of education in recent years. It refers to studying through non-traditional means, primarily online platforms. This approach allows individuals to learn from the comfort of their homes, accommodating various lifestyles and circumstances.

One significant advantage is its flexibility; learners can access courses at any time without strict schedules. Additionally, it enhances accessibility for those unable to attend conventional schools due to geographical constraints or personal commitments.

However, distance learning also presents challenges. Technical issues, such as unreliable internet connectivity, are common hurdles. Moreover, the absence of face-to-face interaction can hinder social engagement and collaboration among students.

Enhanced communication tools could mitigate the lack of personal interaction, making this method even more effective.
