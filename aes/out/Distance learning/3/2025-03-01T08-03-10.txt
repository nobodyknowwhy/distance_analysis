**Understanding Distance Learning**

Distance learning is a method of education where students learn without meeting in a traditional classroom. It began in the 1960s when universities like MIT and Harvard started offering courses over the phone.

Now, it has evolved with online platforms such as websites, video conferencing tools, and forums, making learning more accessible than ever before.

Choosing distance learning offers convenience, allowing students to study at their own pace from home. This flexibility helps manage work or family commitments effectively.

However, challenges remain, like limited internet access for some students and the lack of regular class meetings, which can make staying motivated difficult.

Despite these hurdles, distance learning has become more popular recently due to factors like remote work and the impact of the pandemic on education. As technology advances, its role in education will grow even more significant.
