Distance learning is a method of education where students do not meet in person with their teachers and classmates. Instead, they use technology, such as computers or the internet, to access lessons remotely.

This approach has been growing more popular over time. It started in the early 20th century when people began using new ways to communicate and learn outside traditional schools. After the COVID-19 pandemic, many schools changed to online learning because of health concerns.

Distance learning allows students who live far from schools or cannot attend classes due to disabilities to continue their education. However, it can be challenging because students have to manage their own schedules and stay focused without the guidance of a teacher and classmates.

Despite these challenges, distance learning has its benefits, such as flexibility and access to materials from different countries. It also allows people with busy lives or physical disabilities to attend school from home.

In conclusion, distance learning is becoming more common because it gives students more opportunities to learn while facing many challenges along the way.
