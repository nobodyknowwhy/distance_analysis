Distance learning refers to a method of education where students learn without being physically present in a traditional classroom. This approach can be conducted online or through other digital means such as CDs or DVDs.

One significant advantage of distance learning is its flexibility, allowing students to attend classes from any location and at their own pace for exams. This is particularly beneficial for older students who may have family responsibilities or work commitments that make attending a traditional classroom challenging. It also offers convenience by eliminating the need for commuting between locations.

However, distance learning presents some challenges. Internet connectivity can be an issue, especially in remote areas, which might hinder accessing course materials and submitting assignments. Additionally, managing study time without supervision can prove difficult, potentially leading to procrastination or disengagement.

In conclusion, while distance learning has its benefits like flexibility and accessibility, it also faces challenges such as internet issues and effective time management. Whether this method is suitable depends on individual circumstances and the ability to maintain motivation and structure despite the limitations.
