**Distance learning** refers to the method of studying in which students do not meet with instructors and classmates in a traditional classroom setting. Instead, they usually study at home or another location through structured programs or independent work.

In this system, lessons are often delivered online, either through video lectures, written materials, or interactive modules. Students may communicate with their teachers and peers through emails, discussion boards, or phone calls. Despite its challenges, distance learning has become increasingly popular because it offers flexibility for students who may have family obligations, health issues, or other reasons that make attending a traditional classroom difficult.

One of the main advantages of distance learning is that it allows students to learn at their own pace. They can review lessons as many times as they want and complete assignments when they are ready. However, some students may find it harder to stay focused without the constant supervision of a teacher or classmates in a physical classroom.
