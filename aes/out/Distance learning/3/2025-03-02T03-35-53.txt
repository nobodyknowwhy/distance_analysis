Distance learning refers to education where students do not meet regularly in a physical classroom but instead learn through other means such as correspondence courses or online platforms. It has become more popular recently, especially with schools and universities offering it during the COVID-19 pandemic.

One advantage of distance learning is that it gives students more flexibility. For example, someone working full-time might find distance learning a good way to continue education without missing important deadlines or responsibilities.

Despite these challenges, many people believe that distance learning has a future in education because of its flexibility and potential to reach students who might otherwise not have access to quality education.
