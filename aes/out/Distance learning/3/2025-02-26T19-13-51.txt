Distance learning is a method of education where students don't attend classes in person. Instead, they use technology to access lessons online. This means watching videos or reading materials on a computer or smartphone.

One benefit of distance learning is that it gives students more flexibility. They can watch lessons at any time and as many times as needed. This is especially helpful for students living far from the school. It also allows people in remote areas to access education they might not have been able to otherwise due to lack of internet or resources.

However, distance learning isn't without challenges. Some students find it hard to stay focused because they can't see their teachers or classmates. Internet issues and technical problems can also be frustrating. Additionally, without a physical classroom, it's harder for teachers to help students when they're stuck with homework.

To overcome these challenges, reliable internet connection is essential. Students should use devices that are stable and not too old. Sometimes, having someone to assist with tasks like uploading assignments or explaining concepts can help keep learning going smoothly.
