Distance learning refers to educational programs conducted without students physically being present in a traditional classroom setting. It has become increasingly popular recently, largely due to technological advancements like the internet and the disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

One of its key advantages is flexibility, allowing students to study at their own pace, which is particularly beneficial for those with demanding jobs or families. Additionally, it broadens access to education, enabling individuals in remote areas to continue their studies without the need for extensive travel. This can be especially advantageous for students who otherwise might struggle to attend classes regularly.

Despite these benefits, distance learning also presents challenges. Technical issues such as unstable internet connections or unfamiliarity with online platforms can hinder progress. Furthermore, privacy concerns arise when sharing personal information over the internet.
