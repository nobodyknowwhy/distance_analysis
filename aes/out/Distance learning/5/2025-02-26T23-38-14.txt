**Distance Learning: An Overview**

Distance learning refers to educational methods where students engage in learning activities without face-to-face classroom meetings. This innovative approach has evolved significantly since its inception in the early 20th century but gained substantial momentum with the advent of the internet.

The origins of distance learning can be traced back to the early 1900s when the first courses were conducted via telegraph and cable cars, reaching students on opposite sides of the country. By the 1970s, the development of educational television further expanded its reach, allowing more students access to higher education. The latter half of the 20th century saw exponential growth as the internet became a primary tool for delivering courses online.
