Distance learning is an educational method where students interact with teachers and other students via non-traditional methods such as written materials, audio or video conferencing, and the internet (Popham, 2019). It has evolved significantly over time due to technological advancements. Initially, it was mainly used in certain regions for specific purposes, but now it is becoming more widespread globally.

One of the main challenges of distance learning is technical issues like connectivity problems or outdated hardware. Additionally, students may face distractions at home while trying to stay focused on their studies. Poor internet quality can also hinder the effectiveness of this method. Another issue is the lack of personal interaction between students and teachers in a physical classroom, which might make it harder for students to ask questions or clarify doubts.

Despite these challenges, distance learning offers several benefits. It provides flexibility, allowing students to learn at their own pace and on their own time. This can be particularly beneficial for students who live in remote areas or have special needs such as disabilities. Moreover, distance learning allows for a wider range of resources and materials to be accessed, which can enhance the overall learning experience.

Overall, while distance learning has its pros and cons, it is an effective way to continue education even when traditional classroom settings are not possible.
