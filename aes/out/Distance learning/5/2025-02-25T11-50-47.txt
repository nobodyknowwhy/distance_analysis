Despite its many benefits, distance learning is not without its challenges. One of the primary concerns is the potential loss of face-to-face interaction, which can sometimes enhance personal motivation and accountability in traditional classroom settings. Another critical aspect of distance learning is its dependence on technology. Consequently, it becomes essential for educational institutions to invest in equitable access to these resources.
