Distance learning refers to studying through a network or computer without attending classes in person. It has become increasingly popular due to various reasons.

Originally, distance learning emerged as a response to the limitations of traditional education systems. However, during the COVID-19 pandemic, it gained momentum as many people were unable to attend school. This shift accelerated its adoption and popularity globally.

One advantage of distance learning is its flexibility. Students can learn at their convenience without being restricted by class schedules or locations. Additionally, it provides equal access to education for individuals who might otherwise struggle to attend traditional schools due to mobility issues or other constraints.

Despite these benefits, some challenges exist. For instance, distractions such as family responsibilities and internet problems can occur while studying at home. Technical issues like connectivity and software malfunctions may also disrupt the learning process.
