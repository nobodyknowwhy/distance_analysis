Distance learning is a method of education that enables students to engage in studies from remote locations instead of attending classes on campus. This mode of learning has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its flexibility and accessibility. For instance, students can access a wide range of online courses offered by universities worldwide, many of which are free or low-cost. Additionally, distance learning often involves recorded lectures and other digital resources, allowing students to review content multiple times without missing live sessions. This feature is especially helpful for students who need to revisit complex topics multiple times.

Another benefit is the ability to balance work and study effectively. However, distance learning also presents some challenges. One of the most common issues is limited interaction with instructors and peers. Without the immediate presence of professors during class hours, students may feel isolated and less motivated to engage in discussions or collaborate on projects. To overcome this challenge, many students establish regular study routines and actively participate in online forums to maintain their learning momentum.
